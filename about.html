Hon Lik

Hon Lik, a Chinese pharmacist, invented the electronic cigarette (e-cigarette) in 2003 as an alternative to traditional smoking. His device used battery-powered heating to vaporize a nicotine solution, offering a less harmful way to deliver nicotine and mimic the act of smoking without the harmful effects of tobacco combustion. His invention has had a substantial impact on public health by providing an alternative to traditional cigarettes, although ongoing research is needed to fully understand the long-term health implications of vaping.
